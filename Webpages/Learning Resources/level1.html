Phishing

Summary

What Is Phishing ?

Phishing is a type of fraud that involves stealing personal information such as Customer ID, IPIN, Credit/Debit Card number, Card expiry date, CVV number, etc. through emails that appear to be from a legitimate source.

Key Takeaways

  • Never click links from unknown senders.
  • Hover over buttons to see the actual destination URL.
  • Check for generic greetings like "Dear Customer."

Fake Emails Fraud

Summary

What is Fake Email Fraud?

Fake email fraud occurs when scammers send emails that look like they are from trusted companies, government agencies, or even your own colleagues to trick you into clicking malicious links or sharing private data. These emails often contain "spoofed" sender addresses that look nearly identical to real ones.

How to Spot a Fake Email

  • Look closely for small misspellings in the email address (e.g., support@g00gle.com instead of google.com).
  • If the email claims your account will be deleted or you won a lottery "immediately," it is likely a scam.
  • Hover your mouse over any button to see the real web address before you click.

Fake Websites

Summary

What are Fake Websites?

Fake websites are designed to look identical to legitimate business or government sites to trick users into entering sensitive financial data. Scammers often use low-quality graphics or slightly altered URLs to deceive visitors who aren't paying close attention.

Key Takeaways

  • Check the URL spelling carefully (e.g., amaz0n.com vs amazon.com).
  • Look for "https://" and the padlock icon, but know that these can also be faked.
  • Be wary of websites offering deals that seem too good to be true.

Weak Password

Summary

The Risks of Weak Passwords

Using simple, common, or short passwords makes your accounts easy targets for hackers using automated guessing tools. A weak password is the digital equivalent of leaving your front door unlocked, granting easy access to your personal data.

Key Takeaways

  • Avoid common sequences like "123456" or "password".
  • Do not use personal info like birthdays or pet names.
  • Length is strength: use longer passphrases when possible.

Password Theft

Summary

How Password Theft Occurs

Password theft involves cybercriminals stealing credentials through methods like data breaches, keyloggers, or phishing scams. Once a password is compromised, attackers can access your emails, bank accounts, and sensitive corporate data.

Key Takeaways

  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
  • Change passwords immediately if you suspect a breach.
  • Use a password manager to generate and store unique logins.

Coffee and Password

Summary

Physical Security Awareness

Just as you wouldn't leave your coffee unguarded in a busy public place, you shouldn't leave your passwords exposed or share them casually. Physical security—like protecting your screen from onlookers or not writing passwords on sticky notes—is just as vital as digital security.

Key Takeaways

  • Never write passwords on notes stuck to your monitor.
  • Always lock your screen (Win+L) when stepping away from your desk.
  • Beware of "shoulder surfers" in public places like cafes.

Digital Footprints

Summary

What is a Digital Footprint?

Every online interaction, from social media posts and likes to search history, leaves a permanent digital trail known as a footprint. This data is collected, stored, and often shared with third parties, impacting your privacy and online reputation.

Key Takeaways

  • Think before you post; the internet never forgets.
  • Regularly review and tighten your privacy settings.
  • Delete old accounts that you no longer use.

Internet Safety Tips for Children

Summary

Staying Safe Online for Kids

Children are particularly vulnerable to online risks, including cyberbullying, identity theft, and exposure to inappropriate content. Learning safe browsing habits and understanding the permanence of online actions helps protect their emotional and physical well-being.

Key Takeaways

  • Never share personal details like your school or home address.
  • Do not agree to meet online "friends" in person.
  • Tell a trusted adult immediately if you feel uncomfortable.

Internet Safety Tips for Parents

Summary

A Guide for Digital Parenting

Parents play a crucial role in guiding their children's digital experiences and monitoring their online safety. Open communication, setting boundaries, and using technical tools like parental controls are essential for creating a safe family internet environment.

Key Takeaways

  • Keep computers in a common area to monitor usage.
  • Use parental control software to filter harmful content.
  • Have regular conversations about online friends and activities.

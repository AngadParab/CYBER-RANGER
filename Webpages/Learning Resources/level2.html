Debit & Credit Card Fraud

Summary

What Is Debit & Credit Card Fraud?

Card fraud involves unauthorized actors obtaining your debit or credit card details to siphon funds or make illegal purchases. This theft often occurs through physical skimming devices on ATMs, data breaches, or phishing links that trick you into revealing your CVV and PIN.

Key Takeaways

  • Cover the keypad when entering your PIN at ATMs or stores.
  • Enable SMS alerts to track all transaction activity immediately.
  • Never share your CVV or OTP with anyone over the phone.

QR Code Scam

Summary

What Is a QR Code Scam?

In this scam, fraudsters trick victims into scanning a QR code under the pretense of sending them a payment, often on marketplace apps. However, scanning the code and entering your PIN actually authorizes a deduction from your account instead of a credit.

Key Takeaways

  • You never need to scan a QR code to RECEIVE money.
  • QR codes are strictly used for sending payments.
  • Verify the identity of the buyer before engaging in transactions.

KYC Fraud

Summary

What Is KYC Fraud?

Scammers pose as bank or telecom officials claiming your KYC (Know Your Customer) details are pending and your services will be blocked. They trick you into downloading screen-sharing apps or clicking links that give them access to your device and banking credentials.

Key Takeaways

  • Banks never ask you to download third-party support apps.
  • Do not click on SMS links threatening account suspension.
  • Visit your local bank branch if you are unsure about your KYC status.

SIM Swap Fraud

Summary

What Is SIM Swap Fraud?

Criminals manipulate mobile carriers into transferring your phone number to a new SIM card in their possession. Once the swap is complete, your phone loses signal, and the attacker receives your OTPs, allowing them to bypass two-factor authentication and access your bank accounts.

Key Takeaways

  • Contact your operator immediately if your phone loses signal unexpectedly.
  • Do not share your 20-digit SIM number with anyone.
  • Avoid sharing your mobile number on public social media platforms.

Online Investment Fraud

Summary

What Is Online Investment Fraud?

Fraudsters create fake investment opportunities promising unrealistically high returns with zero risk to lure victims. They often use professional-looking websites or apps to encourage large deposits, only to vanish or block withdrawals once the money is transferred.

Key Takeaways

  • Be skeptical of "guaranteed" high returns with no risk.
  • Verify the company's registration with financial regulators.
  • Never invest based solely on social media tips or unsolicited messages.

WhatsApp Lottery Scam

Summary

What Is a Lottery Scam?

Victims receive messages on WhatsApp or email claiming they have won a massive lottery prize, often from a well-known brand. To claim the nonexistent reward, the scammer demands an upfront "processing fee" or tax payment, after which they disappear with the money.

Key Takeaways

  • You cannot win a lottery you never entered.
  • Legitimate lotteries never ask for fees to release a prize.
  • Block and report numbers sending unsolicited prize notifications.

Catfishing & Romance Fraud

Summary

What Is Catfishing?

Catfishing involves creating a fictional online persona to lure a victim into a romantic relationship. The scammer builds a strong emotional bond over time before inventing a sudden crisis to ask for money, gifts, or private financial details.

Key Takeaways

  • Perform a reverse image search on profile photos.
  • Be suspicious if they refuse to video call or meet in person.
  • Never send money to an online romantic interest you haven't met.

Matrimonial Fraud

Summary

What Is Matrimonial Fraud?

Fraudsters create impressive profiles on matrimonial sites to woo prospective partners and their families. After gaining trust and promising marriage, they concoct fake emergencies—such as customs detention or medical issues—to extract large sums of money.

Key Takeaways

  • Verify the person's employment and background details independently.
  • Involve family members early in the conversation.
  • Keep financial details private until a formal meeting occurs.

Online Games Fraud

Summary

What Is Online Games Fraud?

Scammers target gamers by offering free in-game currency, rare skins, or cheat codes in exchange for login credentials. This can lead to the theft of the gaming account, linked credit card information, and identity theft.

Key Takeaways

  • Only purchase game items from the official game store.
  • Never share your password or login OTP with other players.
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all gaming accounts.

Sextortion Scams

Summary

What Is Sextortion?

Perpetrators coerce victims into sharing intimate photos or videos, often through video calls on social media. Once they have the content, they threaten to publish it to the victim's friends and family unless a ransom is paid immediately.

Key Takeaways

  • Do not panic or pay the ransom; they often demand more.
  • Stop all contact and block the user immediately.
  • Report the incident to the cyber crime portal and local police.

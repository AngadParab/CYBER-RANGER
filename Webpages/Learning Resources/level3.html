Malware & Malware Attacks

Summary

What Is Malware?

Malware, or malicious software, is designed to infiltrate devices to steal data, damage systems, or spy on user activity. It often disguises itself as legitimate files or hides within free software downloads.

Key Takeaways

  • Avoid downloading software from third-party or cracked sites.
  • Keep your operating system and apps updated to patch security holes.
  • Do not ignore browser security warnings about unsafe sites.

Mask and Antivirus

Summary

Why Use Antivirus?

Just as a mask protects you from physical viruses, antivirus software acts as a critical shield for your digital life. It scans your device in real-time to detect, quarantine, and remove threats before they can cause harm.

Key Takeaways

  • Install reputable antivirus software on both mobile and desktop.
  • Enable "automatic updates" to recognize the newest virus definitions.
  • Regularly scan external drives (USB/HDD) before opening files.

Ransomware Attack

Summary

What Is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a severe type of malware that encrypts your files, making them inaccessible until a ransom is paid. Attackers often target victims through phishing emails or unpatched software vulnerabilities.

Key Takeaways

  • Maintain regular offline backups of your important data.
  • Never open email attachments from unknown senders.
  • Paying the ransom guarantees nothing; prevention is the only cure.

Remote Access Fraud

Summary

What Is Remote Access Fraud?

Scammers trick victims into downloading screen-sharing apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer under the guise of "customer support" or "KYC updates." Once installed, the fraudster gains full control of the device to transfer money out of the victim's accounts.

Key Takeaways

  • Legitimate banks never ask you to install screen-sharing apps.
  • If someone asks for a "code" from an app to fix your phone, hang up.
  • Disconnect the internet immediately if you suspect unauthorized access.

Social Engineering

Summary

What Is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is the art of manipulating people into divulging confidential information. Instead of hacking the software, criminals "hack" the human by creating a false sense of urgency, fear, or trust.

Key Takeaways

  • Slow down; scammers want you to act fast without thinking.
  • Verify the caller's identity through an official channel before sharing info.
  • Be skeptical of unsolicited offers that seem too good to be true.

Cyber Stalking

Summary

What Is Cyber Stalking?

Cyber stalking involves using the internet to harass, intimidate, or monitor a victim persistently. Stalkers may use social media, email, or spyware to track a person's location and private activities.

Key Takeaways

  • Review your social media privacy settings strictly.
  • Disable geolocation tagging on your photos and posts.
  • Keep evidence of harassment and report it to the cyber crime portal.

Cyber Bullying

Summary

What Is Cyber Bullying?

Cyber bullying is the act of using digital technology to threaten, harass, or humiliate others. Unlike face-to-face bullying, it leaves a permanent digital footprint and can occur 24/7, causing severe emotional distress.

Key Takeaways

  • Do not respond to or engage with the bully.
  • Save screenshots of messages or posts as evidence.
  • Report the user to the platform administrator and block them immediately.

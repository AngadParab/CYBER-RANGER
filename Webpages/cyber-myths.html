The Reality Check

Click on a myth to reveal the truth behind it.

"I'm too small to be targeted"

The Myth

Small businesses and individuals think they are safe because they have nothing "worth stealing."

The Reality

Automated bots scan the internet for any vulnerability. Your device can be used for botnets, crypto mining, or as a gateway to larger attacks. Everyone is a target.

"Antivirus software is enough"

The Myth

Installing one antivirus program protects me from 100% of threats.

The Reality

Antivirus is just a goalkeeper. You still need defenders: Firewalls, Updates, Strong Passwords, and awareness. Defense in depth is the only real protection.

"Strong passwords are hard to remember"

The Myth

Secure passwords must be random gibberish like 'Xj9#m$2p'.

The Reality

Use Passphrases! A sentence like "MyBlueGuitarPlaysLoud!" is secure and easy to recall. Better yet, use a Password Manager and remember just one master password.

"Macs don't get viruses"

The Myth

Apple computers are immune to all malware by design.

The Reality

While less targeted than Windows historically, Macs can and do get malware. As their popularity grows, so do Mac-specific threats. Security software is essential for macOS too.

"Public Wi-Fi is safe with HTTPS"

The Myth

If a website has a green lock (HTTPS), nobody on the coffee shop Wi-Fi can see my data.

The Reality

HTTPS encrypts the content, but sophisticated attacks can strip this encryption or trick you. Public Wi-Fi is inherently risky. Use a VPN for true privacy.

"Incognito mode makes me anonymous"

The Myth

Private/Incognito browsing hides my activity from everyone.

The Reality

Incognito only stops your browser from saving history. Your ISP, employer, and the websites you visit can still track everything you do.

"Software updates are just for new features"

The Myth

I can skip updates because they just change the look or add features I don't need.

The Reality

Most updates include critical security patches for vulnerabilities that hackers are already exploiting. Delaying updates leaves your door wide open.

"My smart fridge/TV doesn't need security"

The Myth

Hackers don't care about my smart bulbs or thermostat, so I don't need to secure them.

The Reality

IoT devices are often the weakest link. Hackers use them as a "backdoor" to enter your home network and access your computer or bank details.

"Everything in the cloud is automatically safe"

The Myth

If I save my files to Google Drive or iCloud, they are 100% secure from everyone.

The Reality

Cloud providers secure the infrastructure, but you are responsible for your password and access settings. If your account is hacked, your cloud data is gone too.

"Email attachments from friends are safe"

The Myth

If I know the sender, I can safely open any link or file they send me.

The Reality

Attackers often hijack real accounts to spread malware. If a friend sends a weird link or unexpected file, verify it with them through another channel (call/text) first.

"Only geniuses can be hackers"

The Myth

Hackers are super-coders in hoodies typing green code in a dark room.

The Reality

Most cybercriminals use automated tools bought on the dark web. They don't need to be geniuses; they just need you to make one simple mistake.

"I'll know immediately if I've been hacked"

The Myth

If my computer is hacked, it will crash, show a skull on the screen, or act weird instantly.

The Reality

Modern malware is designed to be silent. Hackers want to stay hidden to steal data or use your bandwidth for months without you noticing.

"Mobile phones are safer than computers"

The Myth

I don't need to worry about security on my phone, it's not a real computer.

The Reality

Phones are just pocket computers with cameras and GPS. They face unique threats like malicious apps, SMS phishing (Smishing), and unsecured Wi-Fi risks.

"I can easily spot a phishing email"

The Myth

Scam emails always have bad grammar, typos, and come from weird addresses.

The Reality

AI tools now write perfect scam emails. Hackers also spoof legitimate logos and use "typosquatting" domains (like `g0ogle.com`) that look 99% real at a glance.

"Two-Factor Authentication is too hard"

The Myth

It takes too long to type in a code every single time I log in.

The Reality

Most apps only ask for 2FA on new devices. It adds seconds to your login but stops 99.9% of account hacks. It is the single best security step you can take.

"I have nothing worth stealing"

The Myth

I'm not rich and I don't have government secrets, so why would anyone hack me?

The Reality

Your digital identity is valuable. Hackers can open credit cards in your name, use your email to scam your friends, or hold your family photos ransom.

Your Security Toolkit

Essential habits to stay cyber-secure

MFA Everywhere

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication on every account that offers it.

Auto-Updates

Set your OS and apps to update automatically. Patch those holes!

Verify Senders

Check the actual email address, not just the display name.

3-2-1 Backup

3 copies of data, 2 different media types, 1 stored offsite (cloud).
